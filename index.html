Understanding the Protection Factor Curve

This curve demonstrates how the Protection Factor rewards early members in a collaborative ownership model. The mathematical formula e^((number_of_members - position) × α) creates an exponential advantage for those who joined earlier.

Key Insights:

  • Position 1 (Founder): Always receives the highest protection factor, growing exponentially as more members join
  • Early Members: Get substantial protection bonuses that reflect their early risk-taking
  • Latest Member: Always has factor 1.0 (no bonus) - serves as the baseline
  • Gini Coefficient (α): Controls curve steepness - higher values create bigger gaps between early and late joiners

The curve becomes steeper and more pronounced as the group grows, ensuring founders maintain their advantage even in larger collectives.
